Hello! My name is Ethan Mathieu and I’m a sophomore at Yale studying Computer Science.
Thank you for taking the time to visit my website and learn a little bit more about me.


I like to build things.
CS has always been a passion of mine. Some of my fondest childhood memories are of programming lego robots and making games on Scratch. Tinkering around was just fun.
As I grew older, though, I realized that I had the most fun when the task wasn’t solely dominated by the technical, but also the creative and abstract. I’m drawn to CS because it’s more of an art form than anything. It’s a blank slate—a canvas for me to impart my own ideas on, except this canvas actually has function. There’s always something more to learn, but you are rewardedwith the experience from your past triumphs. And, uniquely, you can easily bring your friends along for the ride and make something cool and useful together.
These reasons are, ultimately, why I love developing.


Currently, I am working as an Undergraduate Research Assistant for the Efficient Computing Lab @ Yale and as a General Developer for the Yale Computer Society. However, these aren't my only CS endeavors, as I am constantly working on my own side projects. The ones that I thought were the coolest are under the project tab.

Also, please do not hesistate to reach out to me using the Contact form if you have any questions about me or my work!
